# Solace
### *a soundtrack for your memories.*

---

## Why I built this

My love for music goes beyond the surface. I wanted to create something 
that dives into the feeling of what music does for me and hopefully for 
other people who discover this.

One thing I've always found so profound is the musicality and feeling 
that instrumentals and sound provoke — whether positive or negative. 
Music has become such an integral part of my life and something I turn 
to in times of distress, which is where the name Solace came about.

My love for music and culture played a big part in the making of this 
project and a big reason into why keywords weren't the only way to 
discover music for other people. Music is subjective — so including 
the Spotify API and making a sonic profile identifier was integral for 
the accuracy I wanted this project to depict.

Some platforms also like grouping genres and artists into a specific box. 
I wanted this web app to do those artists justice while also considering 
that music goes beyond labels. It's science and very much an outlet for 
a lot of this generation.

---

## The Problem

Mainstream music platforms consistently flatten genres and artists — 
particularly those rooted in Black music culture. Amapiano gets called 
"African music." Kelela gets filed under R&B. Afrobeats artists with 
completely different sonic worlds get grouped together. Keywords and 
genre tags reflect the biases of whoever built the algorithm — and those 
biases have always underserved certain artists and sounds.

Solace was built to address that. Not with better tags — but by removing 
the reliance on tags entirely.

---

## How it works

Solace approaches music recommendation differently. Rather than relying 
on user-generated tags or popularity metrics, it analyses the actual 
sonic DNA of a track — valence, energy, danceability, tempo and key — 
to match music to feelings, not algorithms.

This matters especially for genres that are often flattened or 
misrepresented by mainstream platforms. Amapiano, afrobeats, afroswing 
and other culturally specific sounds have their own distinct sonic 
fingerprints that keyword tagging consistently fails to capture.

The Discover page exists specifically to surface artists and sounds that 
live outside the algorithm's radar — not because they aren't significant, 
but because significance has historically been measured by a very narrow 
set of cultural criteria.

Music goes beyond labels. So does Solace.

---

## Sonic Profile Research

Before building the mood filters I analysed real songs across multiple 
genres — pulling audio feature scores to identify sonic patterns.

Key findings:
- **Amapiano** has a consistent tempo fingerprint: 110–117 BPM across virtually all tracks
- **Happy** is not determined by major key — euphoric songs often sit in minor keys with high BPM  
- **Chill and sad** exist on a spectrum not in separate boxes — danceability separates them better than tempo
- **Cultural genres** require their own sonic logic — happiness in amapiano is rhythmic not melodic
- **Mode (major/minor)** is less reliable than tempo + energy + danceability combined

---

#critique and web app evolution 
Amapiano presented a particular challenge — and a particular opportunity. A sad amapiano track still 
carries the log drum, still moves the body, still has the danceability markers that algorithms associate with happiness. 
But underneath is an emotional weight that purely metric-based systems miss entirely. This euphoric sadness 
— grief and movement existing simultaneously — is almost a defining characteristic of the genre and reflects 
broader African musical traditions where celebration and sorrow are not opposites.
To address this, Solace builds genre-specific sonic profiles rather than applying universal emotional metrics. Amapiano has its own tier system — chill, mid and party — determined by energy and danceability rather than valence alone.
As Solace evolves, this approach will be extended across as many genres as possible — building culturally informed sonic profiles that reflect how music actually feels within its own cultural context, not how a western-trained algorithm assumes it should.


## Core Features

- **Mood & Memory Input** — describe a feeling, memory or moment in free text
- **Sonic Profiling** — Spotify API matches songs by audio features not tags
- **Artist Discovery** — editorial descriptions, sonic mood tags, 30 second previews
- **Sonic Chart** — visual representation of an artist or album's audio fingerprint
- **Save a Moment** — name your playlist and store it in your personal memory archive
- **Dynamic Theming** — UI responds to genre and vibe of results

---

## Tech Stack

- HTML, CSS, Vanilla JavaScript
- Spotify Web API — audio features, track data, artist info
- Last.fm API — genre tags and artist data  
- Browser local storage — saving memories, no backend needed
- CSS variables — consistent design system across pages
